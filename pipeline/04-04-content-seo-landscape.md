# Stage 4.4 — Content & SEO landscape

Generate `./planmysaas-blueprint/04-04-content-seo-landscape.md`. Read `04-03-distribution-channels.md` and `03-01-competitive-landscape.md` first — the keyword list and quick-win pages must build on whichever content/SEO channel scoring came out of the distribution stage, and the gap analysis must reference the named competitors.

## Output structure

```markdown
# Content & SEO landscape — <Project name>

## Summary
2-3 sentences: the single biggest content gap versus competitors, and the realistic SEO opportunity given a solo founder's bandwidth.

## Content gap analysis vs competitors

For each named competitor from `03-01-competitive-landscape.md`:

| Competitor | Content gap (what they don't cover well) | Opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| ... | ... | 1 line on what this product can own instead |

## Target keywords by funnel stage

15-20 specific keyword/topic phrases a real person would type into a search bar — not generic topics. Group by funnel stage:

**TOFU (informational, awareness)** — 5-7 phrases
| Keyword phrase | Difficulty (Low/Med/High) | Intent |
|---|---|---|
| ... | ... | informational |

**MOFU (comparison, evaluation)** — 5-7 phrases
| Keyword phrase | Difficulty (Low/Med/High) | Intent |
|---|---|---|
| ... | ... | comparison |

**BOFU (transactional, ready to buy)** — 5-7 phrases
| Keyword phrase | Difficulty (Low/Med/High) | Intent |
|---|---|---|
| ... | ... | transactional |

## Content pillars (3-5)

For each: pillar name + 1-line description of what it covers and which persona/funnel stage it primarily serves.

## Quick-win pages to build first

A prioritized list mixing:
- **Comparison pages** ("<This product> vs <named competitor>") — one per top competitor from `03-01-competitive-landscape.md`
- **Alternative pages** ("best alternatives to <named competitor>")
- **Free calculator/tool pages** relevant to the niche (name the specific calculator/tool and why this persona would search for it)

Each entry: page title · target keyword it captures · 1-line reason it's a quick win (low difficulty, high intent, or fills a named competitor gap).

## Publishing cadence

A realistic cadence for a solo founder (e.g. "1 pillar page + 2 quick-win pages per month for the first quarter, then shift to a comparison/alternative page every 2 weeks once the first pillar is ranking"). State the assumption behind the cadence (hours/week available, whether AI-assisted drafting is in use).
```

## Quality rules

- Every keyword phrase must be something a person would actually type — reject generic single-word topics like "marketing"; require full phrases like "best project management tool for freelance designers."
- Difficulty labels are directional, not pulled from a real keyword-difficulty tool — say "directional" if asked to justify, and reason from competitor content density, not fabricate a numeric score.
- Comparison and alternative pages must name the real competitors from `03-01-competitive-landscape.md`, not a placeholder "Competitor X."
- The publishing cadence must be achievable by one person with no marketing team — reject anything implying a content team or agency-scale output.

## Tone

SEO strategist building a content roadmap a solo founder can actually execute. Specific phrases, no generic topic buckets.
